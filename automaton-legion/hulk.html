Hulk

Classification: Heavy Threat level: Medium to High

Specifications:

Hulks are modular war bots that provide heavy firepower support to deployed automaton squads, there’s usually at least one of these in most contacted patrols in the field. Hulks are covered with heavy armour plating and their design allows for a multitude of powerful weaponry to be installed, the variants this unit can come in are as follows:

  • Bruiser Hulk: Equipped to its left arm is a rocket launcher that fires in seven shot salvos, while its right arm is mounted with an autocannon that fires 20 shots with each burst.

  • Scorcher Hulk: Its right arm is equipped with a heavy flamethrower that it swings from side to side when in use, and its left arm carries a huge circular saw blade that it can and will use to slice any SEAF unit that gets close to ribbons.

  • Obliterator Hulk: Has two rocket launcher arms that fire slower than the Bruiser’s and with fewer number. Due to its lackluster performance, is only deployed to areas with low SEAF presence.

  • Jet Brigade Bruiser Hulk: Has the same installed weaponry as the standard Bruiser, but is also equipped with a heavy-duty jump pack powerful enough to launch its cumbersome weight.

  • Jet Brigade Scorcher Hulk: Equipped with the same weapons as the Scorcher, but with a jump pack.

  • Firebomber Hulk: These infernal creations spew fire to an even greater degree than the Scorcher, equipped with a heavy incendiary grenade launcher in their right arm as well as a powerful flamethrower in their left.

Weaknesses:

All hulks share the same weaknesses, albeit with some differences. Their main weakness lies behind them, with a large heat sink that is vulnerable to light penetration and up. Similar to Devastators, each Hulk has a central control system “head”, but reinforced with some armour. Nevertheless it serves as a weakness, as with a powerful enough shot, can drop the target instantly. Instead of a heat sink in the back, Jet Brigade Hulks’ heavy jump packs can be destroyed, leading to the termination of the Jet Brigade Hulk.

Recommended strategies:

When engaged, try to avoid getting too close to the hulk, as even those without melee weaponry can crush Helldivers underfoot. Aim for the heat sink in the back, once it sustains enough damage, it retains functionality for a small amount of time before it shuts down for good, unless the heat sink is hit with anti-tank. Aim for the red arm connectors with heavy pen to remove the offending appendage. Similar to heat sinks, aim for Jet Brigade jump packs with anti-tank, except this time the Jet Brigade Hulk will terminate immediately, retaining zero functionality after jump pack destruction.

Alternative strategies:

A deadly-accurate Helldiver can make good use of their central control system vulnerability by directing anti-tank at its face, leading to an instant target drop. It is a very small target, so only those who are confident in their sharpshooting skills should attempt.
