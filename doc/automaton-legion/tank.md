# Tank

**Classification:** Heavy
**Threat level:** High

---

## Specifications:
Automaton tanks differ to Super Earth's glorious war machines, in all the wrong ways. They are larger, far less efficient, and more barbaric. Like the rest of the legion, they can be fitted with an array of weaponry. As heavy units, they are heavily armoured, offering absolute protection against small-arms fire. Mimicking our designs, they have a huge chunk of shaped metal for the main hull, along with some menacing spike-like protrusions. Attached to this brutish mass is a pair of large tracks, each with their side portions covered in armour. Atop the main body is an anti-democratic main cannon, designed purely to reduce our rights to dust. Tanks come in these varieties:

* **Annihilator Tank:** Main cannon is a standard high explosive fusion cannon, with the same firepower as automaton outpost tower cannon turrets. The main cannon is also fitted with a smaller automatic weapon that expels a large hail of shots. This variant is also equipped with a repeater turret built into the main hull.
* **Shredder Tank:** The main cannon has been swapped out for a quad-barrelled cycler gun, which it uses to mulch and prepare human corpses for their horrific bio experimentation. This variant lacks the hull-mounted machinegun of the Annihilator variant.
* **Barrager Tank:** Mounted with a large missile battery that it uses to rain heavy duty firepower from above, by launching their missiles upwards into the sky, filling clean air with their corruptive ballistics. Like the Shredder variant, this unit lacks the hull machinegun as well as the standard heat sink weakness.

## Weaknesses:
As tanks, their only form of locomotion can be destroyed and disabled, this requires heavy penetration. Shredders and Annihilators both have the standard heavy unit heat sink weakness that can be exploited to return them to their rightful status - scrap. While not a heat sink in the strictest sense, Barrager tanks feature an engine exhaust in the back, which can be damaged with medium penetration, their missile battery can also be damaged with the same level of armour penetration.

## Recommended strategies:
Destroying their tracks will disable their movement, making them easier targets. Apply appropriate anti-tank capabilities to the main turret or heat sink. Shield relays can prove useful in blocking their shots for a short while.

## Alternative strategy:
If all else fails, a liberal application of "excessive" firepower works wonders.
